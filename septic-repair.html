Warning Signs

Signs Your Septic System Needs Repair

Septic systems rarely fail without warning. Slow drains throughout the house, gurgling sounds in your pipes, sewage odors inside or out in the yard, standing water or unusually lush green grass over the drain field, and sewage backing up into tubs or floor drains are all signs something is wrong. In North Georgia's clay-heavy soil, drainage problems can escalate quickly after heavy rain — a system that limps along all summer can fail completely in a wet week.

The good news: caught early, most problems are repairable. A damaged baffle, a crushed line from vehicle traffic, or roots in a pipe are straightforward fixes. Wait too long and the same problem can saturate and ruin a drain field — turning a repair bill into a replacement bill. If something seems off, it's worth a phone call.

One thing to know: we don't pump septic tanks. ALLCON specializes in repairing and replacing septic systems — if all you need is routine pumping, we'll gladly point you to a reputable local pumping company. But if your system is broken, failing, or at the end of its life, you're in exactly the right place.
